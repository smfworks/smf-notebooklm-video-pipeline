This is a WisdomForge lesson on Marcus Aurelius's teaching: "The universe is change; life is what our thoughts make it. Retire into yourself. The rational principle which rules has this nature: it is content with itself when it does what is just, and so has tranquility."

Structure:
1. Open with the quote and briefly identify Marcus Aurelius — the Roman Emperor who wrote private philosophical notes to himself during wartime and plague
2. Explain the core principle: the Inner Citadel is the part of you that no external event can touch — your capacity to choose your response. Marcus governed an empire yet found his freedom in an internal fortress
3. Show 2-3 specific modern applications: a leader facing a crisis who stays calm by separating what's happening from their judgment of it, a person dealing with criticism who distinguishes between the feedback and their reaction to it, someone navigating a difficult transition who focuses on what they can bring to each day rather than predicting the outcome
4. Close with a practical challenge: tomorrow morning, before checking your phone, spend two minutes identifying one thing you can control today and one thing you cannot — and deliberately let go of the second

Tone: Warm and direct. Speak to a curious adult learner, not an academic. Use concrete examples, not abstractions. Avoid jargon. The goal is insight, not information.

Focus: Make the ancient principle feel immediately useful. The viewer should finish thinking "I can try this today."
