This is a WisdomForge lesson on Epictetus's teaching: "Some things are within our power, while others are not. Within our power are opinion, impulse, desire, and aversion—in short, whatever is our own doing."

Structure:
1. Open with the quote and briefly identify Epictetus — a formerly enslaved philosopher who taught that freedom begins in the mind
2. Explain the core principle in plain language: the dichotomy of control means recognizing what you can and cannot control, and investing your energy only where it matters
3. Show 2-3 specific modern applications: a stressful work situation where you can't control the outcome but can control your preparation, a relationship conflict where you can't control the other person's feelings but can control your response, a daily decision about where to place your attention first thing in the morning
4. Close with a practical challenge: pick one thing today that's outside your control that you've been worrying about, and practice letting it go

Tone: Warm and direct. Speak to a curious adult learner, not an academic. Use concrete examples, not abstractions. Avoid jargon. The goal is insight, not information.

Focus: Make the ancient principle feel immediately useful. The viewer should finish thinking "I can try this today."
